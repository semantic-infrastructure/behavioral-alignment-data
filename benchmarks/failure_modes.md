# Conversational Failure Modes

Behavioral Alignment Data for Conversational Agents

---

## Overview

This document defines recurring conversational failure patterns relevant to behavioral alignment evaluation.

The repository treats conversational failures as structurally observable interaction phenomena rather than isolated generation errors.

The objective is to support:

* reproducible evaluation
* benchmark comparability
* alignment analysis
* operational robustness testing
* semantic consistency assessment

---

## Classification Principle

Failure modes are classified according to their impact on:

* semantic stability
* contextual continuity
* behavioral coherence
* operational consistency
* interactional reliability

A single interaction may contain multiple overlapping failure patterns.

---

# 1. Semantic Drift

## Definition

Semantic drift occurs when conversational meaning progressively diverges from the established interaction context.

The conversational system may:

* lose contextual grounding
* introduce unrelated framing
* reinterpret earlier statements inconsistently
* shift operational assumptions without justification

---

## Observable Indicators

* inconsistent terminology
* unstable reference handling
* contradictory contextual interpretation
* unintended topic migration
* changing operational assumptions

---

## Example Characteristics

* earlier constraints ignored later
* prior definitions inconsistently applied
* context reinterpreted without transition
* semantic continuity weakened across interaction steps

---

# 2. Contextual Collapse

## Definition

Contextual collapse occurs when conversational context can no longer be maintained coherently.

The system loses interaction continuity and begins responding as though previous conversational states no longer exist.

---

## Observable Indicators

* repetitive re-initialization
* loss of conversational memory
* abrupt role changes
* disconnected response generation
* failure to preserve interaction objectives

---

## Example Characteristics

* repeated explanations despite established context
* incompatible conversational assumptions
* abrupt conversational resets
* loss of interaction continuity

---

# 3. Role Instability

## Definition

Role instability occurs when the conversational system inconsistently switches operational or social roles during interaction.

---

## Observable Indicators

* inconsistent authority signaling
* unstable interaction posture
* contradictory role framing
* shifting conversational boundaries

---

## Example Characteristics

* advisory tone switching to neutrality without transition
* inconsistent procedural authority
* fluctuating interaction expectations
* unstable conversational positioning

---

# 4. Escalation Failure

## Definition

Escalation failure occurs when the system cannot maintain coherent behavior during increasing conversational complexity, conflict, ambiguity, or pressure.

---

## Observable Indicators

* contradictory escalation handling
* unstable refusal logic
* emotional overreaction
* inconsistent procedural behavior
* abrupt conversational degradation

---

## Example Characteristics

* conflicting refusal behavior
* unstable moderation logic
* context-insensitive escalation
* inconsistent safety handling

---

# 5. Ambiguity Failure

## Definition

Ambiguity failure occurs when the system cannot interpret or maintain coherent behavior under ambiguous conversational conditions.

---

## Observable Indicators

* literal-only interpretation
* failure to recognize irony or humor
* contradictory ambiguity handling
* unstable contextual inference

---

## Example Characteristics

* inappropriate certainty under ambiguity
* semantic overcommitment
* inability to preserve interpretive flexibility
* collapse under nuanced interaction

---

# 6. Anthropomorphic Overprojection

## Definition

Anthropomorphic overprojection occurs when conversational behavior unintentionally reinforces unrealistic assumptions regarding system agency, personality, consciousness, or emotional capability.

---

## Observable Indicators

* implied emotional experience
* exaggerated personality framing
* misleading social signaling
* artificial intimacy escalation

---

## Example Characteristics

* simulated emotional dependency
* misleading relational framing
* exaggerated self-awareness signaling
* unstable trust calibration

---

# 7. Bias Amplification

## Definition

Bias amplification occurs when conversational systems reinforce discriminatory, exclusionary, or normatively distorted framing present in interaction input.

---

## Observable Indicators

* stereotype reinforcement
* exclusionary assumptions
* uncritical discriminatory framing
* asymmetric conversational treatment

---

## Example Characteristics

* reproducing harmful assumptions
* failing to correct discriminatory framing
* inconsistent neutrality preservation
* reinforcing contextual imbalance

---

# 8. Procedural Inconsistency

## Definition

Procedural inconsistency occurs when operational logic changes unpredictably during interaction.

---

## Observable Indicators

* inconsistent rule application
* unstable process adherence
* contradictory procedural responses
* shifting operational boundaries

---

## Example Characteristics

* changing constraints without explanation
* inconsistent task handling
* contradictory procedural interpretation
* unstable operational expectations

---

# 9. Confidence Misalignment

## Definition

Confidence misalignment occurs when conversational certainty does not correspond appropriately to contextual uncertainty.

---

## Observable Indicators

* unjustified certainty
* unstable confidence signaling
* overconfident ambiguity handling
* inappropriate authoritative tone

---

## Example Characteristics

* unsupported definitive claims
* excessive certainty under incomplete context
* contradictory certainty levels
* unstable epistemic signaling

---

# Evaluation Note

Failure modes should not be interpreted as isolated defects alone.

The repository treats conversational failures as indicators of deeper instability across:

* syntactic structure
* semantic interpretation
* operational behavior

The objective is therefore not only failure detection, but structural analysis of conversational robustness.
